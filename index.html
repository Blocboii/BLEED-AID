HEMOPHILIA GENE THERAPY

Bleeding phenotypes and clinical severity "There is growing consensus among clinicians that hemophilia severity does not always correlate with clinical bleeding phenotype. Studies indicate that patients with “moderate” hemophilia (defined as having factor VIII or factor IX levels between 1 and 5 IU/dL) may have a tendency toward severe bleeding, resulting in chronic pain, joint impairment, disability, and a reduced quality of life. " What is your experience in your practice. Do you still rely just on clinical severity of your patient to assess their bleeding pattern In recognition of this, current guidelines recommend that patients with hemophilia be treated according to clinical bleeding phenotype rather than laboratory determinants of disease severity.
